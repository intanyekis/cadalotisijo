Game Of Thrones Cast Members Who Were Not Killed Off The Show- What Are They Doing Today?

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, June 17, 2024

Game of Thrones became one of the biggest, most well-received series of all time, rivaling the success of HBO’s other monster hit, The Sopranos. Though the series finally was not exactly what fans were expecting, the show was nonetheless responsible for creating a slew of stars.

RELATED: What Has Nikolaj Coster-Waldau Been Up To Since Playing Jaime Lannister On Game Of Thrones?

Now that the original series is over, the question as to what the former stars of the show have been up to is a pertinent one. While It’s known what the slain cast members have been up to since their departure from the show, let’s take a look at what the stars that survived the finale have been up to since, shall we? Strap on your armor, sheath your broadsword and hop aboard a fire-breathing dragon; it's time to do this thing.

10 Kristofer Hivju

Kristofer Hivju portrayed wilding leader, Brienne of Tarth-obsessed, Tormund Giantsbane on GoT. The character was not only a fan-favorite, but played for some of the greatest laughs whenever on screen. After the end of the series, Hivju would go on to voice the character of Jormungandr on the animated Disney series Duck Tales, and most recently debuted on The Witcher as Nivellen. The Norwgian actor is also set to be featured in the upcoming 2023 film Cocaine Bear and the science fiction comedy Distant.

9 Jacob Anderson

Jacob Anderson has kept busy after portraying the loyal Grey Worm on GoT. Anderson has been featured in both big screen and small screen projects, such as appearing on SNL and starring as Vinder on Doctor Who. Most recently, has taken up the role of Louis in the AMC series Interview With A Vampire. Unfortunately, many fans are pitting Anderson’s Louis against Brad Pitt's interpretation.

8 Gwendoline Christie

Brienne of Tarth herself, Gwendoline Christie, decided to make a move back to the theatrical stage after GoT. Christie portrayed Titania/Hippolyta in A Midsummer Night’s Dream in 2019. On the big screen, the Star Wars: The Force Awakens actress was featured in the comedy drama The Personal History of David Copperfield, and most recently the black comedy Flux Gourmet. Christie is also set to be featured in the upcoming series Wednesday, portraying Larissa Weems.

7 John Bradley

The lovable sidekick to Jon Snow, Samwell Tarly was played by John Bradley to much fan appreciation. Following his years on GoT, Bradley was featured in the 2022 disaster film Moonfall, Marry Me, a 2022 romantic comedy, and family drama The Railway Children Return. On the small screen, Bradley was featured on SNL,Robot Chicken, and will be featured in the upcoming series The Three-Body Problem.

6 Jerome Flynn

One of the show’s fan-favorites, Jerome Flynn, played the part of the Bronn on the show. After the finale, Flynn showed up in films such as John Wick: Chapter 3 – Parabellum. Flynn is also set to be featured in the upcoming Western miniseries 1923. Flynn’s career was quite extensive before he starred in GoT, and It seems like the Tommy Zoom actor is continuing that trend.

5 Isaac Hempstead Wright

Who would have ever guessed that Bran would end up King of the North? Well, he did, and the less said about that the better. Anyhow, the actor that portrayed Bran, Isaac Hempstead Wright, hasn’t been extremely busy after the conclusion of GoT as far as acting roles goes. Wright’s only feature film was after Thrones was 2021’s Voyagers. It appears that the former King of the North is taking a break from acting for the moment. However, Wright did go back to school to study neuroscience at University College London.

4 Maisie Williams

There’s something about being a member of the GoT cast and starring in a Marvel property. Such is the case with Maisie Williams as the former Arya Stark was cast in The New Mutants, which was meant for a theatrical debut but instead landed on Disney+ after Disney purchased Fox. Williams was also featured in the 2020 horror film The Owners. As for upcoming projects, Williams is set to be featured in a slew of projects such as the stop-motion animated short Salvation Has No Name, Sinner V. Saints. Williams will also be featured in the TV miniseries biopic about the life of Christian Dior, The New Look.

3 Sophie Turner

Sophie Turner portrayed the red-headed ruler of the North (for a time) Sansa Stark. Post-GoT, Turner has been featured in such films and TV shows as Do Revenge with Maya Hawke and the HBO Max true crime miniseries The Staircase, as well as the TV thriller Survive in 2019.
