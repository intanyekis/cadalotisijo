Sproing Fitness Shark Tank Founder, Net Worth and Investment

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, August 31, 2024

Sproing Fitness, a company specializing in revolutionary workout equipment, made an appearance on the hit TV show Shark Tank. Although they didn’t secure a deal, this innovative fitness brand has managed to thrive and is currently valued at an impressive $30 million. With specialty treadmills designed to provide excellent foot and back support, Sproing Fitness offers a unique and safer workout experience.

Key Takeaways:

  • Sproing Fitness appeared on Shark Tank but didn’t secure a deal.
  • The company is currently valued at $30 million.
  • Sproing Fitness specializes in workout equipment with exceptional foot and back support.
  • The founders, Paul Toback and Steve Lenz, have extensive experience in the fitness industry.
  • Sproing Fitness reported $12 million in revenue in 2020 and has five studio locations.

What is Sproing Fitness?

Sproing Fitness is a company that specializes in workout equipment designed to protect the user’s body from high-impact workouts. Their main product is a line of specialty treadmills that address the common problems of harsh impact on the ankles and knees. These treadmills have special air cushions under the surface to minimize impact and a built-in harness that supports the user’s weight. The goal of Sproing Fitness is to provide a safer and more supportive running experience.

The Entrepreneurs behind Sproing Fitness

Meet the visionary duo behind Sproing Fitness: Paul Toback and Steve Lenz. With their extensive experience in the fitness industry, Toback and Lenz have revolutionized the way people work out. Paul Toback, the former president and CEO of Bally Total Fitness, brings a wealth of knowledge and expertise to the table. After leaving Bally Total Fitness to pursue his own ideas, Toback joined forces with mechanical engineer Steve Lenz to bring Sproing Fitness to life.

Steve Lenz’s background in mechanical engineering has been instrumental in developing Sproing Fitness’s innovative workout equipment. Together, Toback and Lenz have created a company that is transforming the fitness industry.

Through their dedication, passion, and commitment to providing high-quality fitness solutions, Toback and Lenz have paved the way for Sproing Fitness’s success. Their combined expertise and entrepreneurial spirit have propelled the brand to new heights in the competitive fitness market.

The Pitch on Shark Tank

When Sproing Fitness appeared on Shark Tank, they pitched their idea of a treadmill with a built-in back harness to support the user while running and doing other cardio workouts. The Sharks were impressed by the passion and enthusiasm of the founders but had concerns about the narrow market and the perceived difficulty of using the product. They also felt that the price of each unit was too high. Ultimately, none of the Sharks decided to invest in Sproing Fitness.

The Investment and Current Status

Despite not securing a deal on Shark Tank, Sproing Fitness has successfully maintained its business operations. Currently, the company operates five studios that feature their cutting-edge fitness equipment and dynamic workout programs. Moreover, Sproing Fitness has expanded its reach by increasing sales to other gyms as well. This diversification strategy has further contributed to their growth and success.

In terms of financial performance, Sproing Fitness reported an impressive $12 million in revenue in 2020. This signifies the strong market demand for their innovative fitness solutions. Additionally, the company’s current valuation stands at an impressive $30 million, highlighting its value in the industry.

Although the founders did not secure an investment from the Sharks on the show, the ongoing success and profitability of Sproing Fitness demonstrate their ability to thrive independently. This achievement proves the business’s resilience and the effectiveness of its unique fitness offerings.
